An eigenstate $\ket{\psi}$ of an operator $\hat A$ satisfies the ff.:
$$
\hat A \ket{\psi} = \lambda \ket{\psi}
$$
where $\lambda$ is the eigenvalue.

# Pauli Z-operator example

Solving for the eigenstates in the Pauli-Z operator:
$$
\hat Z = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 &-1\end{bmatrix}
$$
Then, we have the ff. equations:
- $\hat Z \ket{0} = 1 \ket{0}$
- $\hat Z \ket{1} = -\ket{1}$

Thus, $\ket{0}$ and $\ket{1}$ are the eigenstates.
