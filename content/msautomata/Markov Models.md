**Probabilistic Markov models** are FSAs where each transition carries a probability rather than a deterministic label. At each step the system _chooses_ a next state stochastically. The key property is the **Markov property**: the next state depends only on the current state, not history. Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) add a twist — you observe outputs, not states directly, and must infer which state sequence best explains the observations (speech recognition, bioinformatics). Think of it as a PDA where the stack is replaced by a probability distribution over states.
