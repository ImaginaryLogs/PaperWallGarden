A **Tsetlin machine** is a form of [learning automaton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_automaton "Learning automaton") collective for learning patterns using [propositional logic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propositional_logic "Propositional logic").

Where neural networks learn through vast numerical computation — adjusting millions of floating-point weights across layers of matrix multiplication — Tsetlin Machines learn through logic. They construct rule-based propositions from data, arriving at decisions that are transparent, deterministic, and remarkably efficient.
