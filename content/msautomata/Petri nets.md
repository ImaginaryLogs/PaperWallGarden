**Petri nets** shift your mental model from "state per cell" to "tokens in places." You have _places_ (circles) holding tokens, and _transitions_ (rectangles) that fire when their input places have enough tokens — consuming some and producing tokens in output places. This naturally expresses concurrency, resource contention, and synchronization in a way TMs and FSAs can't directly model. They're closer to a concurrent computation model. A classic use: modeling a mutex — two processes competing for a shared resource token.
