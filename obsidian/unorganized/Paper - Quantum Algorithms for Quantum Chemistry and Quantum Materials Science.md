## Chemical Quantum Dynamics: Molecules in Motion

Chemical quantum dynamics moves away from stationary states to track time-dependent electronic and nuclear motion, such as how molecules interact with light or evolve during a reaction.

- **The Semiclassical Wall:** Classical methods either scale well but fail to capture quantum effects (approximate path integrals), or they model wave function dynamics accurately but cannot scale due to the exponential dimensionality or the "dynamical sign problem".
    
- **Nonadiabatic Effects & PCET:** At points where different electronic surfaces cross (conical intersections), the Born-Oppenheimer approximation breaks down, requiring the simultaneous quantum treatment of _both_ electrons and nuclei. This is crucial for phenomena like Proton-Coupled Electron Transfer (PCET) in energy storage and enzymes, where the quantum nature of the proton dictates the reaction.
    
- **The Quantum Challenge:** Even though quantum computers naturally handle time evolution, representing these problems is tough because nuclear dynamics involve near-continuum degrees of freedom that are difficult to discretize into qubits.
