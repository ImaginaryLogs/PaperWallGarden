Quantum chemistry primarily focuses on solving the electronic Hamiltonian of a molecule at fixed nuclear positions (the Born−Oppenheimer approximation) to map out its **Potential Energy Surface (PES)**. 

Knowing the PES is the foundation for understanding chemical reactivity, reaction rates, and product distributions.

# Single vs Multi-reference Problem:
Classical methods (like Density Functional Theory or Coupled Cluster) are highly accurate for "single-reference" problems—where the molecule's ground state is dominated by a single electronic configuration (e.g., simple hydrocarbons). 

## Single Reference Problems:
- Hydrocarbons
- Simple molecules like hydrogen
- Covalent bonds

However, classical computers struggle or fail entirely with **"multireference" problems**, where multiple electronic configurations come into play.

## Multi Reference Problems: 
- Active sites of enzymes (like the eight transition metals in nitrogenase used for nitrogen fixation).
- synthetic transition metal nano-catalysts
- The photochemistry of light-harvesting complex pigments.
