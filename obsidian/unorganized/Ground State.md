# Nature Prefers the Ground State
- **Stability:** It represents the most stable structure of a molecule.
- **Predictive Power:** If you know the ground state energy of a molecule before and after a reaction, you can calculate the **reaction energy** ($\Delta E$). This tells you whether a chemical reaction will happen spontaneously (exothermic) or if it requires energy (endothermic).

From Ground State, we can determine other chemical properties:
- **Equilibrium Geometry:** The exact bond lengths and angles where the molecule is most stable.
- **Force Constants:** How the molecule vibrates, which dictates its infrared (IR) spectrum.
- **Transition States:** The energy barriers a reaction must cross, allowing us to calculate **reaction rates** (how fast a chemical process occurs).

**Classical limits:** For anything beyond ~50 electrons in active orbitals, classical computers run out of memory and time. They have to use approximations (like Density Functional Theory), which often fail for complex systems like transition metals or bond-breaking processes.

**Quantum computers** map the wave function $|\Psi\rangle$ directly onto qubits. An exponential amount of chemical data can be stored in a linear number of qubits, allowing us to find the exact ground state energy efficiently.
